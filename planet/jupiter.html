ü™ê Characteristics

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, a gas giant with a thick atmosphere and no solid surface.

  • Atmosphere: Very thick, composed mainly of hydrogen and helium with ammonia and water vapor clouds
  • Surface: No solid surface
  • Color: White, orange, brown and red caused by atmospheric bands and storms
  • Special Features: Great Red Spot (a giant storm), atmospheric bands, and a faint ring system
  • Moons: 95, 4 of which are the Galilean moons (Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto)
  • Core Composition: Unlike solid planets, Jupiter has a "fuzzy" core made up of a mixture of hydrogen and helium
